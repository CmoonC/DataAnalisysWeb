This graph (as well as all subsequent ones) was created using Jupyter Notebook.
After processing the input data, I plotted a graph showing the relationship between apartment prices and square footage. I added a regression line to the main chart. Examining the outcomes leads to the following conclusions: In about 52% of cases, we observe the dependency of +1 m² of surface area equaling +9,760 PLN. The average price of an apartment in Krakow is around 750,000 PLN, and the average apartment size is 56 m². The standard deviation is around 280,000 PLN for price and approximately 20 m² for surface area. The most expensive apartment is a 1,700,000 PLN.

The situation in Warsaw looks like this:
The regression method accounts for 57% of cases. A shift of 1 m² corresponds to a price adjustment of 13,500 PLN. The average property price is 920,000 PLN, with an average area of 61 m². The deviation is 400,000 PLN in price and 23 m² in area. The most expensive offer is two and a half million PLN.
